## Standard structure: 18 fields distributed across 9 slides

| Slide | Function | Blocks |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 1 | **Cover** | text 1 + text 2 |
| 2 | **Hook** | text 3 + text 4 |
| 3 | **Mechanism part 1** | text 5 + text 6 |
| 4 | **Mechanism part 2** | text 7 + text 8 |
| 5 | **Proof / Evidence** | text 9 + text 10 |
| 6 | **Expansion / Turn** | text 11 + text 12 |
| 7 | **Practical application** | text 13 + text 14 |
| 8 | **Direction / CTA setup** | text 15 + text 16 |
| 9 | **Brand signature + final CTA** | text 17 + text 18 |

---

## Function of each section

- **Hook (slide 2)** — creates tension, raises a contradiction or a surprising data point. Connects directly with the cover headline.
- **Mechanism (slides 3-4)** — explains the why. The engine behind the phenomenon.
- **Proof (slide 5)** — anchored in concrete, verifiable data. Table or explicit numbers.
- **Expansion (slide 6)** — deepens or turns the perspective. Does not summarize what came before.
- **Application (slide 7)** — translates to the reader's real world. Practical cases, examples by niche.
- **Direction (slide 8)** — makes the final turn of the argument and prepares the CTA narratively. Can be a short impactful sentence on the gradient slide.
- **Signature (slide 9)** — not a content slide. The brand in the spotlight, with a short CTA and generous negative space.

---

## Word counts per block

Targets, not strict limits. ±3 word tolerance.

> **V2.5 principle:** less text per slide. The image stops the scroll, the text directs. A carousel is a **visual** piece, not a blog article shoved into squares. If a block is over target, **cut**.

| Block | Target | Function |
|-------|------|--------|
| text 1 | ~5 words | Eyebrow / cover overline |
| text 2 | ~7 words | Main headline |
| text 3 | ~18 words | Hook block A |
| text 4 | ~14 words | Hook block B |
| text 5 | ~14 words | Mechanism p1 block A |
| text 6 | ~10 words | Mechanism p1 block B |
| text 7 | ~15 words | Mechanism p2 block A |
| text 8 | ~22 words | Mechanism p2 block B |
| text 9 | ~18 words | Proof block A |
| text 10 | ~12 words | Proof block B (table or list) |
| text 11 | ~17 words | Expansion block A |
| text 12 | ~13 words | Expansion block B |
| text 13 | ~14 words | Application block A |
| text 14 | ~10 words | Application block B |
| text 15 | ~13 words | Direction block A |
| text 16 | ~11 words | Direction block B (impact line) |
| text 17 | ~6 words | Final CTA, one action only |
| text 18 | 0-4 words | Textual lockup/handle if no logo |

**Estimated total:** ~233 words distributed across 9 slides (further reduction on the CTA).

### The "less is more" rule

- If you need to explain everything, you don't trust the image
- A good slide is one that **promises** and **leaves curious** — not one that delivers everything
- Text is there to **hook and direct**, not to be self-sufficient
- When in doubt between 15 and 20 words → choose 15

---

## Promise-fulfilled rule

If the hook (slide 2) says "three decisions", the deck needs to have three decisions before slide 8. If it raises a contradiction, the mechanism needs to resolve it before slide 6.

R2 validates this explicitly before approving the copy:

- [ ] Every count mentioned in the hook appears in the deck (e.g. "three things" → three things)
- [ ] Every contradiction is resolved in the mechanism
- [ ] Every promise is fulfilled before the direction (slide 8)

---

## Final slide rule (texts 17 and 18)

Slide 9 is the **brand signature**, not an editorial conclusion. The thematic turn must happen on slide 8. On the last slide, the brand appears as visual protagonist and the text enters only as a short CTA.

### Mandatory hierarchy

1. Logo or `{brand_name}` in visual first place.
2. Short CTA in second place.
3. Handle, if it exists, as microtext.

If `brand_has_logo=true`, the logo is horizontally centered and optically between 50% and 56% of the slide height. It can sit slightly below center to feel more premium. It never goes to a corner, lost footer or unintentional side.

If `brand_has_logo=false`, `{brand_name}` turns into a large typographic lockup in the same place as the logo. The brand name is treated as a graphic piece, not as a small caption.

### Allowed CTA

- One action only.
- Up to 8 words.
- Preferably one line; maximum two.
- May use comment, DM, save, download, receive, enter, schedule keywords.

Examples:
- `Comment MANUAL and get the guide.`
- `Save to use on the next post.`
- `DM me to build yours.`

### Forbidden on slide 9

- ❌ Explanatory paragraph.
- ❌ Carousel summary.
- ❌ Rhetorical question.
- ❌ Two long blocks competing with the brand.
- ❌ Small logo in the corner.
- ❌ Generic CTA like "learn more" without a clear action.

### Approved closing

- ✅ Large centered logo + `Comment MANUAL and get the guide.`
- ✅ `{brand_name}` in typographic lockup + `Save to review before posting.`

## Adaptations by slide count

The 9-slide structure is the standard. Possible adaptations when you want to vary:

### 5 slides
```
1: Cover | 2: Hook + Context (Dark) | 3: Proof (Light) | 4: Application + Direction (Dark) | 5: CTA (Light)
```
Hook and context merge. Application and direction merge. Keeps proof as an independent slide.

### 7 slides
```
1: Cover | 2: Hook (Dark) | 3: Mechanism (Light) | 4: Proof (Dark) | 5: Expansion (Light) | 6: Direction (Grad) | 7: CTA (Light)
```
Mechanism becomes a single slide. Application is absorbed into direction or expansion.

### 9 slides (recommended standard)
Full structure as in the initial table.

### 12 slides
```
1: Cover | 2: Hook (Dark) | 3: Context (Light) | 4: Mech p1 (Dark) | 5: Mech p2 (Light) |
6: Main proof (Dark) | 7: Secondary data (Light) | 8: Expansion (Dark) | 9: Practical case (Light) |
10: Application (Dark) | 11: Direction (Grad) | 12: CTA (Light)
```
Mechanism expands to 2 slides. Proof expands to 2 slides. Adds a specific practical case before the general application.

**Invariable rule:** the last 3 slides always make a narrative transition to the CTA. The CTA is never the only closing slide without setup.

## Slide tags (short, uppercase, slide function)

Each slide carries a small tag at the top signaling its function. Examples per section:

- Slide 2: `THE PROBLEM` · `THE PHENOMENON` · `WHAT HAPPENS`
- Slide 3: `WHAT THE DATA SHOWS` · `WHY IT HAPPENS`
- Slide 4: `THE MECHANISM` · `HOW IT WORKS`
- Slide 5: `THE NUMBERS` · `THE PROOF` · `THE DATA`
- Slide 6: `WHAT THIS MEANS` · `THE TURN`
- Slide 7: `IN PRACTICE` · `FOR ANY NICHE`
- Slide 8: `THE FINAL RULE` · `THE DIRECTION`
- Slide 9: no functional tag. Use only the logo or `{brand_name}` + short CTA + discreet handle.

Tags are **functional**, not decorative. They must inform the slide's content type, not be a slogan.

---

## Anatomy of the slide-6 turn

Slide 6 is the carousel's pivotal point. Before it: the reader is learning. After it: the reader is being led to action.

**Valid turn mechanisms:**

1. **Amplified implication:** "this means the problem is bigger than it seems"
2. **Reframing:** "the game isn't what you thought — it's something else"
3. **Resolved contradiction:** the paradox raised in the hook now makes sense
4. **Priority inversion:** "what seemed secondary is what matters"

**Invalid mechanisms (which kill the carousel):**

- ❌ Repeat the mechanism's point in other words
- ❌ Announce the CTA early ("and that's why Human Academy...")
- ❌ Moralize ("the problem is that people don't understand")
