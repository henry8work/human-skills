EDUCATIONAL — The power of Yoga in healing the shoulder

How inverted postures help rehabilitate and strengthen the shoulder area.

Briefing:
Educational post from FT studio about aerial yoga applied to recovery and strengthening of the shoulder joint. The proposal is to show, with a tone that is warm and technical at the same time, that inversions on fabric relieve chronic tension, decompress the rotator cuff, and restore range of motion.

Key messages:
- Inversions in aerial yoga decompress the shoulder joint.
- Inverted postures work on strengthening without impact.
- Indicated for those who feel chronic pain, postural tension, or are recovering from mild injury.
- Always with instructor supervision — safety first.

Target audience:
Women 28-50 years old, practitioners or curious about yoga, with a history of shoulder/neck pain from posture or repetitive strain. Those seeking a non-invasive, embodied, and consistent method.

Tone of voice:
Calm, confident, technical without being cold. First person plural when it makes sense ("we work", "we investigate"). No miracle promises.

CTA:
Soft invitation to learn about the studio's therapeutic aerial yoga class.
