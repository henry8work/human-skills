## 0. Doctrine

The biggest source of mediocre branding is not lack of talent — it's lack of exclusion. A mediocre brand does everything it can do. A distinctive brand refuses.

Every creative decision is dual: what to include + what to exclude. Beginner brands think only about the first half. Mature brands think about both.

AI, in particular, gravitates toward the generic by design — it was trained on the market average. Without explicit anti-patterns, it returns average results even when the brief asks for specific. Explicit anti-pattern is the antibody against regression to the mean.

Every decision in this document starts from a premise: **a catalogued anti-pattern is a repertoire of avoided error**. A beginner designer falls into traps that a senior designer recognizes in 5 seconds. An anti-pattern document transfers senior experience to anyone who reads it.

The function of this file is to be **the structural "negative prompt" of the brand**. Every piece produced (manually or via AI) passes through this document's filter before approval.

## 1. Why explicit anti-pattern is strategic

### 1.1 — Identity is built by exclusion

Aesop chose to be apothecary minimalism — it refused exuberant lifestyle. Patagonia chose to be serious outdoor — it refused casual fashion. Apple chose to be premium — it refused low-end.

In each case, the refusal defines the brand as much (often more) than what it offers. A brand without explicit refusal dilutes in 5 years.

### 1.2 — Catalogued anti-pattern accelerates maturity

A designer with 10 years recognizes "tech 2018 purple-blue gradient" instantly. A beginner designer uses it because they find it beautiful. Documenting anti-patterns transfers repertoire.

A brand with documented anti-patterns trains the team faster. Onboarding for a new member includes reading "what we DON'T do" — accelerates the curve.

### 1.3 — AI needs explicit anti-pattern

Image and copy models were trained on the average of the internet. The default is generic. Without an **explicit negative prompt**, AI reproduces mediocrity.

A documented anti-pattern becomes `avoid:` in the image_brief, becomes a system rule in the copy prompt, becomes an audit filter. Without this, AI produces average even with a premium brief.

### 1.4 — Anti-pattern protects in moments of temptation

Every brand goes through moments of temptation:
- "Let's do a post just like the competitor's that went viral"
- "Let's put an emoji in the caption so it looks more casual"
- "Let's drop the price 50% to close the quarter"
- "Let's do a Black Friday with fabricated urgency messaging"

A brand with documented anti-patterns refuses without debate. A brand without anti-patterns debates each case, usually yields.

### 1.5 — Comparative cases accelerate learning

Showing an example of what NOT to do (with analysis) teaches more than abstract principle. "Don't use stock photo" is abstract. "Brand X used a stock photo of a woman smiling at a laptop in a campaign — lost 23% conversion, see the case" is concrete.

The team learns by contrast with real cases.

## 2. The 5 universal anti-patterns

5 cross-cutting anti-patterns that apply across **all** dimensions. Every piece of the brand passes through them.

### 2.1 — Genericness

**Symptom:** if you swap the main subject of the piece for another of the same size, it still works. The brand could be any brand in the category.

**Test:** substitute the brand name with a competitor's name. Does the piece still make sense? If yes, it's generic.

**Correction:** specify until the piece **only fits your brand**. Cite proper names, verifiable data, unique context.

**Example:**
- Generic: "The best experience in photography for your event."
- Specific: "We covered 84 weddings in Porto Alegre in the last 5 years. Documentary 35mm style, no forced posing, delivery in 14 days."

### 2.2 — Empty motivational

**Symptom:** the piece excites without informing. You read it and feel happy, but don't know what to do/learn. There's no data, no action, no next step.

**Test:** after reading the piece, list 3 concrete things you learned/discovered. If the list is empty, it's motivational.

**Correction:** swap excitement for information. Each paragraph needs to carry data, decision, mechanism, or application.

**Example:**
- Motivational: "Believe in your potential. You can achieve everything you dreamed of!"
- Informational: "62% of Brazilian founders give up before 3 years due to lack of cash flow, according to Sebrae 2024. The 38% who remain have in common: planning a runway of 18+ months before starting."

### 2.3 — Terminal cliché

**Symptom:** the piece uses a metaphor or image that has been saturated for years. Lightbulb-idea, gear-strategy, puzzle-solution, corporate handshake, team smiling in the meeting room.

**Test:** does the metaphor appear in 100+ other brands? It's a terminal cliché.

**Correction:** own metaphor, anchored in the brand's specific niche. Or absence of metaphor (just fact).

**Examples of terminal clichés:**
- Visual: lightbulb, gear, puzzle, handshake, tech purple-blue gradient, photo of keyboard with chart
- Verbal: "at the end of the day", "in the end", "we live in an era", "in a world where", "the great truth is"
- Commercial: "transform your life", "take your business to the next level", "the secret no one tells you"

### 2.4 — False intimacy

**Symptom:** the piece fakes proximity that doesn't exist. Calls by name 4 times in the same message. "Our dear customer". "We're with you on this journey". "We want to be your partner".

**Test:** would the piece sound strange if sent to a complete stranger? If yes, it's false intimacy.

**Correction:** intimacy only when real. Respectful distance is more honest than fabricated affection.

**Example:**
- False intimacy: "Hi, dear! How are you? Hope you're well! We want to tell you we're thinking of you and we are your partner on this incredible journey! ❤️"
- Respectful distance: "Hi, [name]. There's news that might interest you: [concrete thing]. Details here: [link]."

### 2.5 — Unfulfilled promise

**Symptom:** the hook promises something the content doesn't deliver. Headline says "3 decisions" but only lists 2. "The secret of X" without explaining X.

**Test:** list all the promises of the hook/title. Verify each is fulfilled in the body.

**Correction:** either fulfill all, or rewrite the hook to be honest about what's delivered.

**Example:**
- Hook: "The 5 mistakes every Brazilian brand makes in generative AI"
- Body delivers: 4 mistakes + 1 generic tip
- Result: reader feels defrauded, loses trust

## 3. DIMENSION 1 — Visual anti-patterns

### 3.1 — Composition

- ❌ Centered text in a content piece (only CTA can be centered)
- ❌ 2 elements of the same visual weight competing (broken hierarchy)
- ❌ Image without overlay over text (poor contrast, illegible text)
- ❌ Card inside a card (heavy composition)
- ❌ Table with fewer than 3 rows (disproportionate)
- ❌ Slide with just 1 sentence + tag (looks incomplete)
- ❌ Decorative background pattern competing with content
- ❌ Large border radius in a serious editorial piece (dated)

### 3.2 — Color

- ❌ Primary color in the background of a large text block (tires the eye)
- ❌ 4+ colors in one piece (visual overload)
- ❌ Neon or Y2K color (wrong decade)
- ❌ "Hollywood teal & orange" color grading without narrative purpose
- ❌ Saturated Instagram filters (Lo-fi, Valencia)
- ❌ Exaggerated HDR
- ❌ 5+ different gradients per page
- ❌ Light gray text on white background (broken WCAG contrast)
- ❌ Single color as marker (green=good, red=bad) — colorblind people don't access it

### 3.3 — Typography

- ❌ Sentence case in headline (no visual weight)
- ❌ Uppercase in body paragraph (illegible)
- ❌ 4-5 different fonts in the same piece
- ❌ Handwritten / brush script text in a professional piece
- ❌ Italic in a whole paragraph (tiring, dated)
- ❌ Positive letter-spacing in body (dated)
- ❌ Justified text (creates white rivers, poor reading)
- ❌ Font-size <14px in mobile body (illegible)
- ❌ Line-height 1.0-1.2 in body (tiring)

### 3.4 — Motion

- ❌ Bounce/elastic on main transitions (looks fragile)
- ❌ Strong parallax (3D rotation, large scale) — dated, distracting
- ❌ Auto-rotating carousel (frustrates reading)
- ❌ Auto-playing video with sound (user-hostile)
- ❌ Fake loading spinner (doesn't indicate real progress)
- ❌ Confetti on everyday interaction
- ❌ Glitch transitions (TikTok 2020-2023 cliché)
- ❌ Fade > 500ms (sense of slowness)

### 3.5 — Photography

- ❌ Identifiable stock photo (immediate loss of credibility)
- ❌ Catalog smile
- ❌ Corporate handshake
- ❌ "Pensive" person with hand on chin
- ❌ Lit lightbulb as idea metaphor (terminal cliché)
- ❌ Gears as strategy metaphor (terminal cliché)
- ❌ Puzzle with missing piece (terminal cliché)
- ❌ AI faces with glassy eyes or strange symmetry
- ❌ Tokenized "diverse" model posing (one of each ethnicity)
- ❌ Entire team smiling in the meeting room
- ❌ Photo of a keyboard with a chart on the monitor for tech context

### 3.6 — Video

- ❌ Aerial drone shot without narrative purpose
- ❌ Generic city time lapse at sunset
- ❌ B-roll of "code scrolling on screen" for tech context
- ❌ Gratuitous slow-motion (no narrative)
- ❌ Subtitles in Comic Sans / Impact / decoration font
- ❌ Whoosh sound on every cut
- ❌ Generic stock music (Jingle Punks default)

### 3.7 — Identity

- ❌ Logo rotated, distorted, with strong filter
- ❌ Generator watermark on the final piece
- ❌ "Swipe →" arrows (swipe is native to Instagram)
- ❌ Random decorative emoji
- ❌ Generic stock icons (envelope for email, magnifying glass for search)
- ❌ Generic iPhone mockup in hero (2018-2022 cliché)

## 4. DIMENSION 2 — Verbal anti-patterns

### 4.1 — Forced binary constructions

```
❌ "It's not X, it's Y"
❌ "It was never about X, it's about Y"
❌ "X decreases, Y accelerates"
❌ "While X loses, Y wins"
❌ "Less X, more Y"
❌ "Before: X. Now: Y."
❌ "No X. No Y."
❌ "More than X, less than Y"
```

### 4.2 — Forbidden openings

```
❌ "Today we're going to talk about..."
❌ "In this post you'll learn..."
❌ "Before we start..."
❌ "As you probably know..."
❌ "Many people ask..."
❌ "Everyone has heard of..."
❌ "Have you ever stopped to think..."
❌ "In a world where..."
❌ "We live in an era in which..."
```

### 4.3 — Forbidden headlines

```
❌ "When X becomes Y"
❌ "The rise of X"
❌ "The impact of X"
❌ "Why X is changing Y"
❌ "X: what you need to know"
❌ "Everything you need to know about X"
❌ "The definitive guide to X"
❌ "X changed forever"
❌ "Became" as the main verb
❌ "We reveal" / "We discovered" / "We show"
❌ "Discover how..."
❌ "5 tips for X"
❌ "10 secrets to X"
```

### 4.4 — Forbidden closings

```
❌ "Continue on the next slide →"
❌ "Swipe to see more"
❌ "But there's more..."
❌ "I hope you enjoyed!"
❌ "If you have questions, send me a message"
❌ "Thanks for following along"
❌ "Don't forget to follow"
❌ "If this content helped you..."
❌ "The question that remains is..."
❌ "At the end of the day..."
❌ "In the end..."
```

### 4.5 — Forbidden tics

```
❌ "And that changes everything"
❌ "In a X way" (generic)
❌ "It's clear that X"
❌ "Increasingly"
❌ "Simply"
❌ "Basically"
❌ "In practice" (as opening)
❌ "In summary"
❌ "As we saw"
❌ "The great truth is"
❌ "As X used to say"
❌ "It's not for nothing that"
```

### 4.6 — Data without source

```
❌ "Studies show that..."     → always name the study
❌ "Experts say..."            → always name who
❌ "Many companies..."         → always a number or example
❌ "Most people..."            → always a percentage
❌ "Recently..."               → always a date or period
❌ "In Brazil..."              → always specific data
❌ "Research proves..."        → source + year
```

### 4.7 — Numeric anglicisms

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|---|
| "10+ years" | "more than 10 years" |
| "5x larger" | "five times larger" |
| "2-3 years" | "two or three years" |
| "40k followers" (in editorial) | "40 thousand followers" |
| "100M users" (in editorial) | "100 million users" |

### 4.8 — Forbidden corporate vocabulary

| Jargon | Replace with |
|---|---|
| Ecosystem | system, market, environment |
| Synergy | integration, collaboration |
| Disruptive | breaks pattern, changes the rule |
| Stakeholders | involved parties, interested parties |
| Mindset | mentality, way of thinking |
| Engagement (vague) | result, performance, reach |
| Curation (generic) | selection, criteria-based choice |
| Storytelling | narrative, story |
| Hands-on | practical, hands-dirty |
| Insight (empty) | observation, discovery |
| Pain point | pain, problem, friction |
| Pipeline (non-technical) | flow, conveyor |
| Roadmap (non-technical) | route, planning |
| Dive deep | deepen |
| Think outside the box | (cut — cliché) |
| Game-changer | (cut — cliché) |
| Innovative (without qualification) | (cut — cliché) |
| Revolutionary | (cut — cliché) |
| Unique in the market | (cut — usually false) |

### 4.9 — General tone

- ❌ Guru tone ("you need to do this")
- ❌ Empty motivational tone ("believe in the process")
- ❌ Sales tone in editorial (tiring, loses trust)
- ❌ Technical tone in a piece for a general audience (inaccessible)
- ❌ Paternalistic tone ("we know you don't understand, so...")
- ❌ Second person ("you") in a serious editorial piece

## 5. DIMENSION 3 — Behavioral anti-patterns

### 5.1 — In channel

- ❌ Deleting critical non-spam comments (Streisand effect)
- ❌ Blocking those who complain (turns into a post about the block)
- ❌ Mass positive comments (bot) — always discovered
- ❌ Auto-DM "thanks for following" — standard spam
- ❌ Asking for a review/like in proactive DM (spam behavior)
- ❌ Reposting a customer story without permission (image rights)
- ❌ Sharing a DM screenshot without consent (ethical)
- ❌ Over-"personalize" (calling by name 4x in the same message)

### 5.2 — In crisis

- ❌ Prolonged silence (>6h in level 3+ crisis)
- ❌ Statement written by a lawyer without brand review
- ❌ Blaming a third party (supplier, AI, "system")
- ❌ Mocking the crisis
- ❌ Pretending nothing happened ("a small misunderstanding")
- ❌ Posting content "as if nothing happened"
- ❌ Generic apology without concrete action
- ❌ Defending employees in a flagrant situation (United Airlines 2017 case)

### 5.3 — In commercial conflict

- ❌ Accepting an out-of-scope project "because the client insisted"
- ❌ Giving a discount without strategic justification
- ❌ Extending a deadline "to accommodate" when it means cutting quality
- ❌ Accepting recurring late payment without interest
- ❌ Speaking ill of a competitor publicly

### 5.4 — In onboarding/offboarding

- ❌ Hiding the cancellation channel (anti-practice)
- ❌ Maintaining automatic billing after cancellation (unethical + illegal)
- ❌ "Are you sure?" button on cancellation (undue friction)
- ❌ Reactivation with extra discount "just for you" (insults those who stayed)
- ❌ Transactional email with institutional greeting ("Dear customer")

## 6. DIMENSION 4 — Editorial anti-patterns

### 6.1 — Agenda

- ❌ Agenda without an angle (just "talks about X")
- ❌ Agenda repeated from what we've covered in the last 30 days
- ❌ Agenda that fits any brand in the niche (generic)
- ❌ Agenda on a taboo topic (defined in the DNA)
- ❌ Empty motivational agenda ("believe in yourself")
- ❌ Agenda with clickbait ("the secret no one tells you")
- ❌ Agenda following "trend of the day" without brand alignment

### 6.2 — Format

- ❌ "10 tips for X" (saturated format, low lift)
- ❌ "Everything you need to know about X" (generic)
- ❌ "Hard sell" carousel without editorial (tiring)
- ❌ Reel just of "visual transition" without content
- ❌ Live without a clear agenda
- ❌ Newsletter copy-pasted from blog (doesn't respect the format)

### 6.3 — Tone

- ❌ Guru tone in editorial
- ❌ Empty motivational tone
- ❌ Sales tone in editorial
- ❌ Technical tone in a piece for a general audience
- ❌ Paternalistic tone

### 6.4 — Structure

- ❌ Unfulfilled hook promise (universal anti-pattern 5)
- ❌ Closing that summarizes instead of pivoting
- ❌ Absence of CTA when it makes sense
- ❌ Generic CTA ("learn more", "check it out")
- ❌ Density below expected (empty paragraphs)

## 7. DIMENSION 5 — Commercial anti-patterns

### 7.1 — Price

- ❌ Fabricated urgency ("last spots!") when it's not true
- ❌ Black Friday discount >50% (burns margin + signals inflated price)
- ❌ Price upon request without justification (creates conversion friction)
- ❌ Recurring "special offer" (becomes normal price)
- ❌ Comparing prices directly with a nominal competitor (unethical)
- ❌ Hiding the total price until the last step of checkout

### 7.2 — Scope

- ❌ Accept everything "to close" (team burnout, quality drops)
- ❌ Sell a feature that doesn't exist yet ("we're developing it")
- ❌ "All-inclusive" without defining limits (client abuses)
- ❌ Accept project with poorly cooked brief without guiding
- ❌ Accept impossible deadline to close the sale

### 7.3 — Partnership

- ❌ Accept partnership with a brand whose values conflict (reputation)
- ❌ Accept barter without equivalence calculation (under-valued)
- ❌ Sponsor a brand without due diligence (becomes a crisis later)
- ❌ "Strategic partnership" announced without real substance

### 7.4 — Post-sale service

- ❌ Run support only via bot (frustrates)
- ❌ Hide the cancellation channel (anti-practice)
- ❌ Reactivation with extra discount (insults retained customers)
- ❌ Maintain automatic billing after cancellation (unethical + illegal)
- ❌ Ask for a 5-star review in exchange for something

## 8. The 7 most common anti-patterns in Brazilian brands

Patterns observed specifically in small and medium-sized Brazilian brands. Documented to recognize + avoid.

### 8.1 — "We will be a reference" as positioning

Aspirational positioning without concrete proof. "We will be a reference in [category]" is not positioning — it's desire. Real positioning requires a demonstrable current differentiator.

**Correction:** define what you do **today** that justifies leadership. If you don't have it, there's no positioning yet.

### 8.2 — Motivational coach disguised as a brand

A personal brand that sells itself as a "consultant of [thing]" but whose communication is 90% motivational. "Believe in your potential", "you can do it", "don't give up".

**Correction:** distinguish personal brand from motivational coach. A brand has a method, framework, proof of results, scope refusal. A motivational coach sells emotion.

### 8.3 — "2010 website" aesthetic

Lobster + Pacifico typography, blue-green gradient, 2010s flat icons, "100% satisfaction guaranteed" badges, auto-rotating carousel on the home, email popup at second 0.

**Correction:** visual audit with contemporary reference (Stripe, Linear, Vercel, Aesop). Update visual system in 30-60 days.

### 8.4 — "Humanized service" only in the campaign

The brand promises "humanized service" in the campaign, serves with a bot in DM, copy-pastes in customer service, no real follow-up.

**Correction:** brand promise aligned with brand behavior. If it promises human, it delivers human. If it can't deliver human at scale, it changes the promise.

### 8.5 — Performative brand purpose

The brand adopts a social cause (sustainability, diversity, mental health) without aligned operation. Posts about Yellow September but pressures the team to burnout. Posts about Black History Month but has 100% white leadership.

**Correction:** brand purpose only with aligned operation. Without operation, it's greenwashing/woke-washing — burns when discovered.

### 8.6 — "Broad niche" as segmentation

"We serve women between 25-45, class B/C, throughout Brazil, interested in quality of life." That's 60 million people — it's not a niche, it's demographics.

**Correction:** specific persona + clear JTBD. Declared anti-persona.

### 8.7 — Logo redesign as "strategic evolution"

The brand redesigns the logo every 3-5 years, announcing it as "strategic evolution" — without having changed positioning, product, or culture. Usually accompanied by "new era" communicated in a LinkedIn post.

**Correction:** rebrand is a last resort, not a first. Renewal respects accumulated heritage. Visual change without strategic change is internal vanity.

## 9. How to audit an existing brand — structured method

### 9.1 — Phase 1: Evidence collection (1 week)

Collect representative samples of each dimension:

- **Visual:** screenshots of site, recent posts (60 days), institutional presentation, current ads
- **Verbal:** captions, emails, site copy, ads
- **Behavioral:** screenshots of DMs (anonymized), responses to comments, crisis communication if any
- **Editorial:** agendas from the last 30 days, formats used
- **Commercial:** sales page, checkout flow, post-sale

### 9.2 — Phase 2: Application of the 5 universals (2-3 days)

For each sample, scan the 5 universal anti-patterns:

1. **Genericness** — does it work for any brand in the category?
2. **Empty motivational** — does it excite without informing?
3. **Terminal cliché** — does it use a saturated metaphor?
4. **False intimacy** — does it fake non-existent proximity?
5. **Unfulfilled promise** — does the hook deliver what was promised?

Mark each sample with which anti-patterns appear.

### 9.3 — Phase 3: Application of the 5 dimensions (3-5 days)

For each sample, scan the corresponding dimension:

- Visual: anti-patterns 3.1 to 3.7
- Verbal: anti-patterns 4.1 to 4.9
- Behavioral: anti-patterns 5.1 to 5.4
- Editorial: anti-patterns 6.1 to 6.4
- Commercial: anti-patterns 7.1 to 7.4

### 9.4 — Phase 4: Diagnosis (2-3 days)

Compile a report with:

- **Top 5 most frequent anti-patterns** (with count and examples)
- **Most affected dimensions** (visual? verbal? operational?)
- **Systemic patterns** (if the same anti-pattern appears in all dimensions = cultural problem)
- **Critical anti-pattern** (the one that most threatens immediate reputation)
- **Prioritized recommendations** (3-5 concrete actions + deadline)

### 9.5 — Phase 5: Correction plan (1 week)

- Short term (1-30 days): fix the critical anti-pattern + the 5 most frequent
- Medium term (30-90 days): update templates, train the team
- Long term (3-12 months): redesign systems (visual/voice/behavior) if necessary

## 10. Comparative cases (anonymous, but recognizable)

### 10.1 — Generic Brazilian B2B SaaS

**Anti-patterns observed:**
- Hero with purple-blue gradient (dated visual)
- Headline "The complete solution for [sector]" (genericness)
- Stock photo of a diverse smiling team (terminal cliché)
- CTA "Learn more" (verbal anti-pattern 4.4)
- Pricing "Upon request" (commercial anti-pattern 7.1)
- DM with bot even in first contact (behavioral anti-pattern 5.1)

**What would work:**
- Hero with a unique identifiable piece
- Headline with a real differentiator
- Authorial photo or absence of photo
- Specific CTA ("I want to see a 5-min demo")
- Transparent pricing
- Human service in first contact

### 10.2 — Personal brand of a motivational coach "AI consultant"

**Anti-patterns observed:**
- Aspirational positioning ("the best AI consultant in Brazil")
- 90% motivational communication ("you can do it!", "don't give up!")
- Persona "every entrepreneur who wants to grow" (genericness)
- Forced "luxury" aesthetic (photo of founder in front of a borrowed Lambo)
- False intimacy ("friends!", "dears!", calling by name 4x in the same DM)

**What would work:**
- Positioning anchored in specific method
- Informational communication (cases, frameworks, data)
- Specific persona (real profile)
- Aesthetic aligned with real positioning
- Adult, professional tone

### 10.3 — Mid-range fashion e-commerce

**Anti-patterns observed:**
- 8 colors on the home (visual overload)
- 4 different carousels (auto-rotating)
- Fixed promotional banner blocking the top
- Product photo on infinite white without lifestyle
- "LAST PIECES!" on products with high stock (fabricated urgency)
- Newsletter with subject "🔥🔥🔥 DON'T MISS IT!"

**What would work:**
- Restricted palette (3 colors)
- No auto carousel (1 static hero)
- Contextual promotional banner (only where it makes sense)
- Product photo + lifestyle context
- Urgency only when real
- Specific subject lines

## 12. Operational checklist

### Before approving any piece

1. Apply the 5 universal anti-patterns (section 2)
2. Apply anti-patterns of the relevant dimension (sections 3-7)
3. List detected anti-patterns (with textual/visual citation)
4. Decide: approve / adjust / reject
5. If adjusting: indicate concrete correction

### Before auditing an existing brand

1. Evidence collection by dimension (Phase 1)
2. Scan of the 5 universals (Phase 2)
3. Scan of the 5 dimensions (Phase 3)
4. Structured diagnosis (Phase 4)
5. Prioritized correction plan (Phase 5)

### Before defining the anti-patterns section of the DNA.md

1. Capture anti-patterns specific to the brand's category
2. Capture competitors' anti-patterns (to avoid)
3. List with reason to avoid (it's not arbitrary)
4. Document 5-10 anti-patterns per dimension
