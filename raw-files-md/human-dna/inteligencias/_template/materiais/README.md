# 📁 Materials — where you drop the brand's raw material

This is the folder where you drag everything you have for your brand (or things that serve as inspiration). The more material here, the better the DNA we'll build together.

**You don't need to fill in everything now.** Start with what you have on hand and add as we talk.

---

## The 7 sub-folders

### 1. logos/
Everything from your logo — versions, variations, isolated symbol. Accepts PNG, SVG, JPG, PDF.

### 2. tipografias/
Font files you use or want to use (`.ttf`, `.otf`, `.woff`). If you don't have any yet, no problem — during the conversation I can suggest some.

### 3. referencias-visuais/
Inspiration images: Pinterest screenshots, the magazine cover that stuck with you, screenshot of a site you love, photo that defines the mood. No rule — drop whatever comes.

### 4. exemplos-do-meu-tom/
Texts of yours that represent well how your brand speaks. It can be an Instagram caption you liked, an email you wrote, a website text, anything in `.txt`, `.md` or screenshot.

### 5. concorrentes/
Screenshots or links from brands that occupy the same space as you — not to copy, but so we understand what you want to do **differently**.

### 6. anti-referencias/
The **opposite** — things you do NOT want to look like. As important as inspiration. Drop here what bothers you in your niche.

### 7. outros/
Anything that doesn't fit in the previous ones: old deck, draft brand manual, Word doc with ideas, link to a podcast that defines the vibe.

---

## How the system uses this

When you start the conversation with the Maestro (running `claude` here in the folder), it will ask about the brand naturally. As the conversation moves on, it will ask you to drag things into the folders above — or if you've already dropped them in, it will open and look at them (sees images, reads text, identifies fonts) and use them to build your DNA.

**You never need to explain what each file is.** Drop it in, it understands.

---

## Privacy

Everything you drop here stays **only on your computer**. Nothing goes to the internet automatically. At the end of the process, the Maestro will ask if you want to archive an organized copy somewhere more accessible (Notion / Google Drive) — then you decide.
