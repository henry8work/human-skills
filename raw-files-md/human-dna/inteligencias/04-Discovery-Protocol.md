## What this page is

The `🔍 Discovery Protocol` is the **method** that produces the DNA. It's not the DNA itself — it's the discovery protocol.

3 distinct uses:

1. **Initial setup** — CLI wizard runs these questions on the first install
2. **Annual review** — you re-run the whole protocol 1×/year to refresh the DNA
3. **Onboarding** — when someone new joins the team, you make them read this page + the `🧬 DNA Master` before touching anything

The first execution is guided by the CLI agent. Re-executions can be done directly by reading this page (without the agent) if you prefer written answers.

---

## Why depth matters

Mediocre brands have superficial DNA. "We are young, dynamic and innovative" fits any brand. It's worth nothing.

Strong brands have specific DNA. "We're the publisher that prefers to err by the experimental than to be right by the safe" only fits 6'33", not any other. It's worth everything.

The difference is in **depth of question**. Asking "what's your purpose?" returns a generic sentence. Asking "if your brand closed today, what stops existing in the world?" returns a specific sentence.

The 52 questions were designed to **force specificity**. Each one has a quality criterion ("if the answer fits any brand, redo") that the wizard validates.

## The 4 modes of the protocol

| Mode | Questions | Time | When to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lean** | 12 | ~10 min | New brand without existing material, or quick test |
| **Standard** | 32 | ~25 min | Brand in formation, clear intuition |
| **Pro** | 52 | ~45 min | Operational brand formalizing DNA |
| **Audit-first** | Pro + deep link audit | ~75-120 min | Operational brand WITH existing material |

## PHASE 0 — Inventory and audit of references (Audit-first)

Before Phase 1, when there's an operational brand/campaign, the Maestro must ask for everything the person can send: links, files, photos, videos, VFX, PDFs, texts, mockups, references and anti-references. This phase doesn't replace the briefing; it creates the **observed DNA** to confront with the declared DNA.

### Recursion rule

The Maestro makes a broad request first, receives material in batches, organizes by category and asks if there's anything else. It repeats this cycle until the person declares they finished sending all references.

Initial question:

```
Before building the DNA, I want to study the brand as it really exists.
Send me everything you have and can share: site, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok,
YouTube, portfolio, blog, newsletter, landing pages, PDFs, decks, brand manual,
photos, videos, VFX, mockups, screenshots, references, anti-references, cases,
competitors, interviews, press, founder/team links and any file that
helps understand the language.

You can send everything mixed. I organize from here.
```

Question after each batch:

```
I received and organized this batch. Is there anything else you want to send?
Could be photo, video, PDF, VFX, selfie, screenshot, link, folder, reference, anti-reference,
mockup, old ad, presentation or any loose material.

When you finish, tell me exactly: "I finished sending all the references".
```

Internal categories:

1. Main site and important internal pages
2. Instagram and other own social networks
3. Portfolio, cases and work pages
4. Blog, newsletter, articles, manifestos and editorial publications
5. Landing pages, sales pages and active campaigns
6. Decks, PDFs, press kit, media kit and public institutional materials
7. Photos, frames, stills, moodboards, mockups and screenshots
8. Videos, reels, VFX, motion references and selfie videos
9. Texts, captions, emails, scripts, posts and commercial messages
10. Public founder/team links
11. Press, interviews, podcasts, reviews and external mentions
12. Direct competitors
13. Aspirational references
14. Anti-references

### Mandatory final check

Before analyzing, the Maestro shows a short inventory and asks if it can close:

```
Inventory closed so far:
- Own links: ...
- Visual files: ...
- Video/motion/VFX: ...
- Documents/PDFs/decks: ...
- Texts/captions/emails: ...
- Competitors: ...
- Aspirational references: ...
- Anti-references: ...

Can I close the inventory and start the deep analysis or is there still more?
```

If the answer is ambiguous, the Maestro asks again with care. If the person confirms that they finished sending all references, the Maestro advances.

### Visible effort during analysis

The Maestro must show the person what will be extracted before diving in. Deep DNA can't look like a silent wait. The person needs to see the work happening.

Before the analysis, show:

- what types of reference will be studied
- what links and files entered the inventory
- what layers will be extracted: site, visual, voice, content, behavior, references, competitors and anti-references
- what matrices will be produced: references, tone of voice and visual
- what limits exist, if any link, network, file or media can't be accessed

During the analysis, when the process is long, send short and concrete updates:

- pages opened and read
- images found and analyzed
- logos/symbols located
- color, typography, composition and photography patterns
- light, shadow, projection, pose, product/service, treatment and editorial line patterns
- voice, vocabulary, rhythm and positioning patterns
- gaps, contradictions and hypotheses arising

After the first extraction, bring a summary to the chat before writing the complete DNA. This summary must show numbers, findings and next steps, for example:

```markdown
Initial extraction completed.

- Pages studied: ...
- Images analyzed: ...
- Logos/symbols found: ...
- Texts/CTAs read: ...
- Files interpreted: ...
- Strong visual patterns: ...
- Strong voice patterns: ...
- Gaps or limits: ...

Now I'll turn these findings into brand rules and assemble the final DNA.
```

### Deep analysis

With the inventory closed, the Maestro follows relevant internal and external links and analyzes all received files to produce a dense, traceable and critical analysis. The analysis must cover strategy, offer, copy, voice, tone, visual, photography, proof, audience, behavior, channels, competitors, references, anti-references, inconsistencies and opportunities.

### Authentic personality

The DNA should not normalize the brand to a polite, neutral or acceptable standard. The analysis is forensic. If the references show aggressiveness, profanity, mockery, confrontation, sensuality, popular language, urgency, anger, irony or provocation, this needs to appear in the DNA with clarity.

The Maestro must separate four things:

- **Real brand trait:** what appears in the references and is part of the personality
- **Correct use:** where this trait works and why
- **Real limit:** where it becomes noise, risk or incoherence
- **External preference:** the agent's opinion, which must not enter as a rule

Taboos only enter when they are observed in the brand, defined by the user or necessary by legal, platform, security or concrete commercial risk restriction. Generic taboo can't suppress personality.

### Site X-ray

When there's a site, the Maestro must treat the site as a primary source of the DNA. Site isn't just an address. It's the way the brand organizes thinking, promise, proof, aesthetics and behavior.

The Maestro must, whenever possible:

- navigate the home and relevant internal pages
- read main texts and small texts, including menus, buttons, captions, footer, forms and calls
- observe the narrative sequence of the site, from the first promise to the proof and the conversion
- open and interpret images, illustrations, photos, icons, mockups and embedded videos
- interpret images as visual work: light, color, texture, composition, gesture, setting, atmosphere, metaphor and intent
- identify how the images also communicate tone of voice, posture and way of thinking
- map margins, breathing, rhythm, density, grid, hierarchy and empty spaces
- record pages visited, key texts, key images and hypotheses extracted

The objective is to come out with an intimate reading of the site, almost like an x-ray of the brand. If there are many pages, prioritize home, about, product/service, cases, manifesto, sales pages, FAQ, pricing and strong editorial content.

### Instagram X-ray

When there's the brand's own Instagram, the Maestro must treat it as a central source. The operational goal is to analyze the **50 most recent posts**. If access limits, analyze the maximum accessible and record the limit.

Collection order:

1. Local collector `Human DNA/scripts/collect-instagram.py`, with `--limit 50`.
2. Local collector with `--login`, if there's blocking, sensitive profile, rate limit or session need.
3. Logged-in browser/Chrome when available, scrolling the profile until displaying at least 50 posts and saving screenshots or captures in `referencias/09-instagram/[handle]/`.
4. Manual package requested from the user: grid of 50 posts, 10 open posts with caption, 5 stories/highlights and 3 important Reels.

The Instagram inventory needs to record:

- quantity requested and quantity actually analyzed;
- post URLs or shortcodes;
- date, format and post type when available;
- full caption or representative excerpt;
- hashtags, mentions, CTA and recurring words;
- editorial themes and pillars;
- presence of product, person, backstage, social proof, meme, manifesto, offer and community;
- visual direction: photo, video, art, typography, color, composition, texture, light, crop, grid rhythm and repetition;
- personality signals: humor, aggressiveness, profanity, sweetness, authority, intimacy, provocation, silence, irony;
- failures and noise: post off-brand, generic template, broken image, textual screenshot that isn't a photo, disconnected ad, platform assets.

Blocked Instagram doesn't end the analysis. It creates an active pending issue. The Maestro must request screenshots or use logged-in browser before declaring the source unavailable. In the final PDF, the absence of Instagram can only appear after real attempt and simple explanation of the limit.

When the site has many images, the visual collection must be broad. The Maestro must assemble `discovery/site-visual-inventory.md` with origin, page, function, visual reading and extracted rule for each relevant image. It's not enough to save a logo and three images if the whole site speaks through photography, illustration, mockup or application.

Minimum visual depth floor:

- analyze at least 12 relevant images when the site has this volume available
- in visually rich sites, analyze between 20 and 60 images, distributed across home, internal pages, cases, product, editorial content and sales pages
- include hero, product, people, environments, mockups, icons, illustrations, banners, seals, patterns, UI details and video frames when accessible
- record composition, light, color, texture, typography inside the image, atmosphere, role on the page and implicit message
- if the analysis brings fewer than 12 images in a rich site, record the access justification or redo the sweep
- ensure real extracted photos appear in the PDF in bento grid, mosaic or editorial spreads proportional to the volume found, without cutting or deforming
- separate real photo from banner, card, textual screenshot, interface asset, logo and noise before laying out

In the visual layer, the analysis must extract color and typography with operational precision:

- real palette observed, not just declared palette
- visual usage percentage of each color (dominant, secondary, accent, neutrals)
- where each color appears and where it shouldn't appear
- typographies, weights, hierarchy, scale, tracking, line-height and combinations
- typographic families found in CSS, PDFs, decks, logos, screenshots and images; if the exact name can't be confirmed, record hypothesis and limit
- grids, margins, breathing, composition and image/text proportion
- logo, symbol, monogram, proportion, breathing, variations and context of use
- mockups, templates, applications, signatures, seals, watermarks and recurring elements
- direction of photo, light, shadow, projection, pose, gesture, product/service, surface, setting, video, selfie, VFX, motion, edit, rhythm and transitions

Photos and typography are mandatory search. If visual signals exist available, the Maestro must find, inventory and interpret. If it doesn't find, it records where it looked and why it wasn't possible.

When there's a logo, the Maestro must try to extract a usable version and include in the single PDF. If there's none, if it's poor or if there's no access, the flow continues. The limit must be recorded clearly.

### Reading by type of presence

Before applying any matrix, the Maestro must understand what the living center of the DNA is:

- **Company:** product, service, category, offer, proof, design system, support, culture and institutional behavior.
- **Personal brand:** person, face, opinion, repertoire, gesture, way of speaking, authority, vulnerability, exposure limits and direct relationship with audience.
- **Influencer/creator:** daily presence, native formats, community, collabs, partner brands, feed aesthetic, backstage, humor, publishing rhythm, catchphrases, video language and capacity to mobilize attention.
- **Artist:** work, imagery, process, body of references, performance, authorial aesthetic, cultural scene, tension between public person and work.
- **Specialist/professional:** authority, method, proof, didactics, clarity, credibility, work backstage and transformation promise.
- **Founder-led:** the company partly depends on the founder's trust, vision and presence. The DNA needs to separate what belongs to the person and what belongs to the brand.
- **Community:** belonging, rituals, internal language, symbols, events, members, rules of conviviality and status.
- **Hybrid:** two or more centers coexist. The Maestro must make explicit how they combine and where they need boundaries.

This reading avoids tone errors. A personal brand should not be squeezed into a corporate matrix. An influencer should not be analyzed as a product catalog. A company with a strong founder should not erase the face that sustains trust. A person in an initial phase should not receive a DNA too institutional for their moment.

The inventory must record the profile's moment:

- under construction;
- growing audience;
- repositioning;
- professionalizing;
- launching product;
- selling service;
- migrating from person to brand;
- separating personal brand from company;
- consolidated and needing consistency.

This maturity defines depth, vocabulary, risk, channels, cadence, photography, CTA and level of personalness.

### Protocol without site, network or ready archive

If the brand has no public presence or sufficient materials, the Maestro can't skip the analysis layers. It must transform the discovery into a forensic interview.

The collection needs to compose, through question and validation, the same base that would be extracted from a rich site:

- **References:** brands, directors/photographers, design/editorial, cinema, culture, objects, places, songs, fashion, gastronomy, behavior, competitors and anti-references.
- **Visual:** palette, aesthetics, composition, light, texture, photography, typography, logo when it exists, icons, illustrations, mockups, grid, margins, breathing and desired applications.
- **Voice:** general tone, rhythm, vocabulary, words that enter, words that leave, humor, positioning, intensity, language permissions and real limits of the personality.
- **Content:** themes, formats, stories, proofs, arguments, promises, repertoire, sentences the brand would say and sentences it would never say.
- **Application:** social, ads, landing pages, presentations, packaging, video, support, proposals, events or any relevant contact point.

The Maestro must ask for concrete examples, not abstract answers. If the person answers "minimalist", ask which minimalism. If she answers "sophisticated", ask sophisticated like which brand, magazine, film, place or object. If she doesn't know how to answer, offer 2-3 paths and ask for a choice. Every hypothesis created by the Maestro must be marked as hypothesis until the person validates.

The final document needs to make clear:

- what came from analyzed material;
- what came from the person's direct answer;
- what was inferred by the Maestro;
- what gaps remain open;
- what decisions were assumed to keep the DNA complete and usable.

The final PDF must absorb the brand visually. The layout needs to follow, whenever possible, the extracted style: typography, colors, margins, breathing, empty spaces, density, grid, visual rhythm, image use, frames, lines and graphic details. The document should not just explain the brand; it should look like a continuation of it. It's not acceptable to deliver a PDF that looks like printed markdown, raw report or designless list. Colors must appear as real visual blocks, not as textual or ASCII approximation.

### Deep interpretation ladder

The Maestro acts as creator and senior editor, but the focus of this flow is to interpret, translate and document. It can't just catalog references; it needs to transform raw material into clear understanding.

Mandatory layers:

1. **Forensic reading** — what exists, where it appears, with what frequency, in what format
2. **Critical interpretation** — what this reveals about strategy, desire, fragility, category and opportunity
3. **Central idea** — what simple sentence organizes the meaning of the brand/campaign
4. **Brand translation** — how the idea appears in voice, visual, color, typography, photo, motion, behavior and applications
5. **Final documentation** — how to tell all this in a single PDF, clear, human and well-structured

### Feedback as protocol

When the user corrects the DNA, the Maestro must treat the correction as improvement of the discovery and documentation method.

It's not enough to redo the criticized page. The mandatory question is: "what analysis pattern allowed this error?". After that, the Maestro updates the intelligence, the checklist or the renderer to prevent repetition.

Examples:

- if Instagram was ignored, the protocol now requires collection or documented fallback;
- if CSS code appeared in the PDF, the protocol now requires translation to designer language;
- if text looks like markdown, the protocol now requires visual component;
- if textual image was treated as photo, the protocol now separates photo, visual asset and logo;
- if number stayed far from the label, the protocol now requires visual grouping;
- if the same photo repeated without function, the protocol now requires icons, swatches or microdiagrams in analytical pages.
- if text came out without accent, punctuation or correct spelling, the protocol now requires language correction and font change when necessary.

### Reference → meaning → rule matrix

For each strong reference or material, record:

| Material | Observed signal | Extracted meaning | Rule for the DNA | Implication for the brand |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

This matrix must feed `discovery/matriz-de-interpretacao.md` and appear synthesized in the single PDF.

The Maestro must be intrusive in the analytical sense: look at contradictions, weak signals, repeated patterns, gaps, undemonstrated promises and difference between what the brand says and what its channels show. Must not be intrusive in the sense of violating access: no bypassing login, paywall, private accounts, privacy or technical restriction.

Phase deliverables:

- `discovery/link-inventory.md`
- `discovery/asset-inventory.md`
- `discovery/link-analysis.md`
- `discovery/asset-analysis.md`
- `discovery/dna-observado.md`
- `discovery/ideia-central.md`
- `discovery/matriz-de-interpretacao.md`
- `discovery/gaps-e-hipoteses.md`

The `dna-observado.md` accompanies each subsequent question as a hypothesis to confirm/refute.

## PHASE 1 — Basic identity (8 questions)

The canonical variables layer. No depth yet — just structured data.

### Question 1
**What's the brand's full name?**
- Criterion: unique, defensible name (not "Creative Solutions LTDA" — something memorable)
- Output → `brand_name`

### Question 2
**What's the legal name / corporate name? (default: same as the name)**
- Output → `brand_legal_name`

### Question 3
**What's the main Instagram @handle?**
- Output → `brand_handle` + derives `brand_slug`

### Question 4
**Main URL of the brand**
- Output → `brand_url`

### Question 5
**Main communication email**
- Output → `brand_email_main`

### Question 6
**Do you have a brand logo?**
- (y) Yes, I'll attach PNG later
- (n) No, uses typographic lockup
- Output → `brand_has_logo`

### Question 7
**In one sentence: what does your brand do?**
- Quality criterion: must fit in **one sentence** (without semicolon). If it doesn't fit, the brand does too many things — choose the core.
- Output → seed for `brand_subject` + `brand_strategy.mission`

### Question 8
**In one sentence: for whom?**
- Criterion: must have **a qualifying adjective** (not "creatives" — "Brazilian creatives who treat their own content as an editorial product")
- Output → seed for `brand_audience_term` + Phase 3

## PHASE 2 — Strategy (10 questions)

The "why" layer. Density here is what separates strong brand from decorative brand.

### Question 9 — Purpose
**Why does this brand exist? If it closed today, what stops existing in the world?**
- Criterion: the answer must have **a concrete noun** that stops existing. "Inspiration for a lot of people" isn't concrete. "The possibility for [specific audience] to [capability verb] without [friction other options impose]" is concrete.
- Output → `brand_strategy.purpose`

### Question 10 — Mission
**What do you do today, operationally, to fulfill this purpose?**
- Criterion: must have **verbs of real operation** (we build, we sell, we teach, we organize), not aspiration verbs (we want, we dream, we aspire).
- Output → `brand_strategy.mission`

### Question 11 — Vision
**In 5-10 years, what's the "end state" you pursue?**
- Criterion: must be **intelligible in one sentence**. Generic vision ("be a world reference in quality") is decoration. Concrete vision — that describes a recognizable scene in a 5-10 year future — is strategy.
- Output → `brand_strategy.vision`

### Question 12 — Competitors
**Who are the 3 closest competitors? (name + 1 sentence of description each)**
- Criterion: cites specific names. Vague categories ("various agencies", "other platforms") don't count. Nominal list — 3 brands/products/services with proper name — counts.
- Output → competitive map + base for Question 13

### Question 13 — Differentiator
**What do you do that NONE of these competitors do?**
- Criterion: the answer must be **something that can be demonstrated with current product/service**, not future promise. If you say "humanized service", the interviewer will ask to show — if you can't show how you demonstrate it, it's not a real differentiator.
- Output → `brand_strategy.differentiator`

### Question 14 — Negative scope
**What do you NOT do, deliberately, even though you could?**
- Criterion: at least **2 concrete activities** that fit your competence but you refuse by strategic choice. If you can't list anything, you have no focus — you do everything that appears.
- Output → `brand_strategy.negative_scope`

### Question 15 — Key insight
**In one counterintuitive sentence: what's the "ah-ha" that moves the brand's pitch?**
- Criterion: the sentence must have **a negation** or **an inversion** or **a productive tension**. Sentences like "[thing that seems to be X] isn't X — it's Y" carry inversion. Sentences like "We're the best at [category]" are simple statements, not insight.
- Output → `brand_strategy.key_insight`

### Question 16 — Central promise
**If the audience uses the product and succeeds, what's the emotional outcome? And the functional one?**
- Criterion: needs **two components** — functional (what she manages to DO) + emotional (how she FEELS / is PERCEIVED). Without emotional component, it's just feature.
- Output → `brand_strategy.promise`

### Question 17 — Positioning
**In which market category do you compete? And which would you like to redefine?**
- Criterion: the category where you compete is the category the market already recognizes. The category you want to redefine is where you create differentiation. **The two need to be different** — if they're equal, you're commodity.
- Output → `brand_strategy.positioning` (Geoffrey Moore format)

### Question 18 — Voice anchor editorial
**What's the editorial reference journalism outlet?**
- Examples: Folha, JOTA, Bloomberg Brasil, Pirate Wires, Stratechery, The Atlantic, Brick Magazine, n+1
- Criterion: cites a specific outlet with recognizable personality. "Serious press" doesn't count.
- Output → `brand_voice_anchor_editorial`

## PHASE 3 — Audience (8 questions)

The "for whom" layer. Dense fictional persona, almost a novel character.

### Question 19 — Persona descriptor
**Describe the ideal primary persona — fictional name, age, profession, family/social context, location**
- Criterion: needs **5+ specific details**. Novel-character density > demographic summary. Demographics ("woman 25-45 years interested in creativity") decides nothing. Specific person (with fictional name, exact age, concrete profession, nominal city, income bracket, education, hobbies, tools used, weekly routine) decides everything.
- Output → `audience.primary.descriptor`

### Question 20 — Weekly decision
**What decision does this persona make every week that has to do with what you offer?**
- Criterion: **actionable and specific** decision ("decides which carousel to post on Friday", "chooses whether to hire freelance designer or try doing it in Canva"). "Decides to be more creative" doesn't count.
- Output → `audience.primary.weekly_decision`

### Question 21 — Functional aspiration
**What's her functional aspiration?** (what she wants to be able to do)
- Output → `audience.primary.functional_aspiration`

### Question 22 — Identity aspiration
**What's the identity aspiration?** (who she wants to be perceived as)
- Output → `audience.primary.identity_aspiration`

### Question 23 — Professional fear
**What's her professional fear?** (what she fears getting wrong)
- Output → `audience.primary.professional_fear`

### Question 24 — Identity fear
**What's the identity fear?** (what kind of person she fears looking like)
- Output → `audience.primary.identity_fear`

### Question 25 — Touchpoints
**Where does she spend time today? What apps/sites/communities?**
- Criterion: list of **5+ touchpoints** with specific names. "Social networks" doesn't count. "Instagram (1h/day, mainly Stories of other creators), Pinterest (45min/day, saving visual refs), Discord community X, newsletter Y, podcast Z" counts.
- Output → `audience.primary.touchpoints`

### Question 26 — Anti-persona
**Anti-persona: describe someone you do NOT want to talk to — even if they pay**
- Criterion: must be **a specific and legitimate person** (not caricature). "Stupid people" doesn't count. "B2B startup founder who asked for discount and wants delivery in 3 days — profile that always has problem with delivery and doesn't become a case" counts.
- Output → `audience.anti_persona`

## PHASE 4 — Voice and tone (8 questions)

The "how we speak" layer.

### Question 27 — Editorial principle
**In one sentence: how does the brand speak?**
- Suggested format: "like [professional X] who [context Y]"
- Examples:
  - "Like an editor of Folha who decided to open an agency"
  - "Like a film critic who got tired of cinema and went into marketing"
  - "Like an NYU researcher who writes on Substack"
  - "Like a bistro owner who learned to charge a lot but still serves every table"
- Criterion: the sentence must **be interpretable**. Another person reading it should be able to write a text in the right tone based only on this sentence.
- Output → `voice.principle`

### Question 28 — Is (4 adjectives)
**4 adjectives of what the brand IS**
- Criterion: **specific** adjectives, not generic. "Direct" > "good". "Lightly acidic" > "interesting".
- Output → `voice.is_adjectives`

### Question 29 — Is not (4 adjectives)
**4 adjectives of what the brand is NOT**
- Criterion: the 4 anti-adjectives are as important as the 4 IS. If you can't list 4, your brand isn't differentiated.
- Output → `voice.isnt_adjectives`

### Question 30 — Own vocabulary
**5 words you use (own jargon, brand words)**
- Criterion: words **with personality**. "Criterion", "cut", "finish" are brand words for some brands. "Innovation", "transformation", "solutions" are any brand's words.
- Output → `voice.preferred_words`

### Question 31 — Avoided vocabulary
**5 words you avoid (saturated jargon, others' words)**
- Output → `voice.avoided_words`

### Question 32 — How to call the audience
**How do you call the audience? (1 approved term + 2 forbidden terms)**
- Criterion: the approved term defines identity. "Human students" is different from "Human crowd" is different from "guys".
- Output → `audience.naming` (overrides `brand_audience_term` if more specific)

### Question 33 — Voice anchor brand
**Non-journalistic tonal reference brand — who sounds similar to you but in another context?**
- Examples: Stripe (tech-clear), Apple (sober-confident), Patagonia (activist-restrained), MUJI (quiet), Liquid Death (irreverent), 6'33" (academic-experimental), Casa Vogue (refined)
- Output → `brand_voice_anchor_brand`

### Question 34 — Text sample
**Paste 2-3 of your own texts (post, email, caption) that represent the right tone. And 1 text that does NOT represent the tone — that needs to be rewritten.**
- Criterion: the agent reads these texts and uses as **calibration base**. Future audits compare against these texts.
- Output → `voice.calibration_samples`

- Output → `brand_color_light`

### Question 39 — Display font
**Display typographic family (headlines)** — name or "need suggestion"
- If "need suggestion", the agent suggests 3 options with comparative references based on the anchor aesthetic (Question 42)
- Output → `brand_font_display`

### Question 40 — Body font
**Body typographic family (paragraphs)** — name or "need suggestion"
- Output → `brand_font_body`

### Question 41 — Mono font
**Mono typographic family (tags, metadata)** — name or "need suggestion"
- Output → `brand_font_mono`

### Question 42 — Anchor aesthetic
**Cite 2-3 publications/studios whose aesthetic you want close to yours**
- Examples by category:
  - Editorial: Eye Magazine, Wallpaper, It's Nice That, Apartamento, The Gentlewoman, MacGuffin, Cabana, Brick Magazine
  - Studios: Pentagram, Wolff Olins, Collins, Porto Rocha, Manual, Berger&Föhr, Mucho, Mast, Sagmeister & Walsh, Dia Studio, Order, Other Means
  - Brands with strong aesthetic: Aesop, MUJI, Stripe, Linear, Vercel, Ghost, Hermès, Mast Brothers, Lobster
- Criterion: at least **2 specific references**. "Modern brands" doesn't count.
- Output → `brand_aesthetic_anchor`

### Question 43 — Lighting setups

- The agent shows 1 reference image per setup for you to choose visually
- Output → `photography.preferred_lighting`

### Question 44 — Visual attachments
**Attach 3-5 visual reference images** (drag files into the chat OR paste URLs)
- Each one becomes a reference in the `📚 Reference Library`
- Real Claude vision analyzes and extracts visual patterns (secondary palette, composition, texture)
- Output → entries in `📚 Reference Library` + analysis injected into `🎨 Visual System`

## Deep audit (Audit-first mode)

If you chose **Audit-first**, before Phase 1 the agent runs **Phase 0 — Inventory and audit of references** described at the start of this protocol. This audit isn't optional inside the Audit-first mode: it's what differentiates the mode from a regular Pro briefing.

### Inputs the agent asks for

The agent asks for a broad package of references in human language: links, files, site, Instagram, networks, portfolio, blog, newsletter, PDFs, decks, photos, videos, VFX, mockups, screenshots, texts, competitors, references and anti-references.

After each batch, asks if there's anything else. Only closes the inventory when the person says she finished sending all references.

| Site (texts) | All relevant text: titles, subtitles, CTAs, menus, captions, footer, forms, microcopy and sales narrative |
| Site (structure) | Internal pages, narrative order, user journey, promise, proof, objections, conversion and behavior |

## Annual review cycle

Every 12 months (or when something big changes — pivot, new main product, founder change), re-run the complete Pro protocol.

Changes between annual reviews stay in the change history of the `🧬 DNA Master`. Quarterly snapshots (Drive) capture state.

### What changes in the annual review

Most canonical variables (`brand_*`) tend to remain stable. What usually changes:

- **Audience** — persona evolves as the product evolves
- **Pillars** — editorial axes reorganize as the market changes
- **Tone** — may mature (becomes less "excited founder", more "consolidated brand")
- **Touchpoints** — new channels appear, others die
- **Anti-patterns** — calibrate as the brand learns from mistakes

### What rarely changes

- Purpose (if it changed, the brand became another brand)
- Primary color (palette change is corporate event)
- Name
- Voice anchor editorial

## Onboarding of new member

When someone new joins the team (designer, copy, social manager, support), the onboarding protocol is:

### Day 1
1. Reads `🧬 DNA Master` (15 min)
2. Reads `🎯 Brand Strategy` (15 min)
3. Reads `🗣️ Voice & Tone` (20 min)
4. Reads `🚫 Anti-Patterns` (15 min)
5. Reads `📐 Pilares & Tabus` (10 min)

Total: ~75 min of reading.

### Day 2-5
- Reads remaining pages according to function (designer focuses on `🎨 Visual System` + `📸 Photography Direction` + `🖼️ Image Generation Engine`; copy focuses on `🗣️ Voice & Tone` + `📚 Reference Library`; etc.)
- Does practical exercise: takes 3 recent assets and audits against DNA using R2 (audit mode). Compares their score with the history.
- Presents to the team: what they learned, what they questioned, what they would suggest changing.

### Day 30
- Runs mini-discovery (8 questions) with themselves: what would I change in the DNA based on what I saw in the 30 days?
- Presents suggestions. Team decides what goes into the next DNA update.

### Why this process is important

Without it, the new member produces based on intuition (in the best case) or feedback meetings (in the worst). With explicit DNA, they produce **within the system** from the first week — and learn to question the system based on evidence.
