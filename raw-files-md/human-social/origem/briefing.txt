EDUCATIONAL — Why slow coffee tastes different

How brewing method and water temperature change what you actually taste in the cup.

Briefing:
Educational post from Alto Café (fictional specialty coffee brand, used as example) about manual brewing methods. The proposal is to show, with a tone that is warm and technical at the same time, that pour-over extraction highlights acidity and aroma, while immersion methods favor body and sweetness.

Key messages:
- Grind size and water temperature decide what gets extracted.
- Pour-over highlights florals and acidity; French press highlights body.
- You don't need expensive gear to brew noticeably better coffee.
- Freshly ground beans matter more than any single accessory.

Target audience:
Adults 25-45, curious about specialty coffee, who already brew at home but feel the result is inconsistent. People who enjoy learning the "why" behind everyday rituals.

Tone of voice:
Calm, confident, technical without being cold. First person plural when it makes sense ("we tested", "we recommend"). No snobbery, no gatekeeping.

CTA:
Save this post for your next brew, and tell us in the comments which method you use at home.
