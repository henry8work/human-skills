# 01 — Canonical template of DNA.md

> This file is the **complete reference structure** of the `DNA.md` that the Maestro generates in `resultado/DNA.md` at the end of the briefing. It defines exactly what needs to be there, in which sections, with what depth.
>
> **The Maestro reads this file silently** before generating the DNA.md. Doesn't cite it to the person. The person sees the final result in `resultado/DNA.md` and the laid-out material in `resultado/DNA.pdf`.

---

## Deliverable philosophy

The `DNA.md` is the **base of every project**. All content generated for that brand later (text, image, video, page, graphic piece) consults this file. So it must be:

1. **Complete** — covers everything needed to produce a new piece without having to go back and ask the founder
2. **Justified** — every choice comes with **why** (not just "primary is #X" but "primary is #X because [reason]"). Justification enables coherent decision-making in new situations
3. **Operational** — it's not a poetic manifesto, it's a manual with application rules ("primary appears in CTA, accent in keyword, NEVER as background of a large block")
4. **Specific to the brand** — nothing flat/generic. System built for this brand, not copied from a standard manual
5. **Authorial** — doesn't summarize materials; interprets. The Maestro acts as strategist, creative director and senior editor: extracts tension, picks a point of view, names the territory and creates a system the brand hasn't been able to formulate on its own
6. **Narrative** — the final DNA needs to tell the brand as a clear, well-written and well-proven story. The analysis doesn't need to generate new pieces; it needs to translate the references deeply and show what they reveal

---

## Complete DNA.md structure

### Section 0 — Header

```markdown
# Creative DNA — [name or "brand under construction"]

> Brand master file. Every AI/person who creates brand content reads this file FIRST.
> Generated on [YYYY-MM-DD] by the Creative DNA Maestro · Version 1.0
```

### Section 0.5 — Executive summary

> 200-300 words in prose. Synthesis of the DNA on one page, for quick reading. Who the brand is, how it sounds, how it appears, what it refuses. Whoever reads just this section already has 70% of what's needed.

```markdown
## Executive summary

[Paragraph 1: identity — who the brand is, what it does, for whom]
[Paragraph 1.1: type of presence — company, personal brand, creator/influencer, artist, specialist, founder-led, community, auteur product or hybrid; explain current moment]
[Paragraph 2: how it sounds — editorial principle in one sentence + 3-4 core adjectives]
[Paragraph 3: how it appears — palette + typography + aesthetic in two lines]
[Paragraph 4: what it refuses — 2-3 most critical anti-patterns]
[Paragraph 5: differentiation angle that emerges]
```

### Section 0.8 — Single PDF deliverable

> Mandatory when the user asks for material "like a brand book", "marketing guide", "branding PDF" or any applicable package after the analysis.

```markdown
## Single PDF deliverable

The presentable deliverable must be **a single final PDF**, written like a clear, well-structured essay, but laid out as an advanced editorial presentation:

- `resultado/DNA.md` — full textual source used to render the material
- `resultado/DNA.pdf` — single, editorial, presentable PDF, containing all the analysis on 16:9 pages with slide rhythm

The `resultado/DNA.pdf` must consolidate everything into one material:

- central concept and brand/campaign context
- central idea, main tension and brand territory
- strategic synthesis and positioning
- sources studied and references extracted
- map of references by type: brands, directors/photographers, design/editorial, cinema and culture
- analysis of received links and files
- tone of voice, vocabulary, examples and verbal anti-patterns
- voice reading by category: general tone, rhythm, vocabulary, words to avoid, humor and positioning
- palette, visual usage percentages, contrast and application rules
- typography, hierarchy, grid, composition and spacing
- visual reading by category: palette, aesthetics, composition, light, texture and visual references
- deep photographic analysis: light, shadow, projection, framing, perceived lens, pose, gesture, distance, product, setting, surface, texture and treatment
- deep typographic analysis: families found, function, weight, contrast, scale, hierarchy, lettering, verbal-visual signature and editorial use
- logo, symbol, visual signature and rules of use when they exist
- photographic direction, video, VFX, motion and use of selfie when applicable
- reading of mockups, applications and existing materials
- translation of references into brand rules
- final criteria for marketing, design, social, landing pages, ads and presentations

Auxiliary files may exist as backstage (`discovery/`, images, logs, supporting markdowns), but the user receives and consults **a single final PDF** as brand material. Don't generate `brand-book.pdf`, `palette.pdf`, `typography.pdf` or any separate PDF.

The PDF can't look like a markdown file dumped inside a PDF. It must work like a brand presentation: cover, rhythm, breathing, hierarchy, section pages, real color blocks, visual gallery with caption, logo when it exists, images in editorial composition and text treated like writing, not like a technical dump.

The expected visual standard is **editorial deck**, not report. Each page must be thought of as a slide: one main idea, deliberate composition, clear contrast, image with narrative role, little text per screen and visual continuity between pages. Extra depth goes into the `DNA.md`; the PDF consolidates. Target is 14 to 22 strong pages, expanding only when the real visual archive demands more spreads — never to paginate more text. Never cram text and never inflate pages: fewer strong pages always beats more weak pages.

All PDF content is from the analyzed brand. The template examples appearing in this template and in the discovery protocol (brand names, sentences, colors) are only reasoning reference — they can't appear in the PDF as if they were the brand. When the material allows, the document delivers the three depth matrices filled in with the real brand: References (brands, directors/photographers, design/editorial, cinema, culture), Tone of voice (general tone, rhythm, vocabulary, avoid, humor, positioning) and Visual (palette, aesthetics, composition, light, texture, references). Each cell brings extracted reading, meaning, practical rule and limit — never a copied example.

When the user sends images with lists, tables, maps or examples of structure, the Maestro must first read their content and extract the logic. It should not copy the layout of those images as visual direction, unless the user explicitly asks. If the image is a content checklist, it becomes analysis structure, not aesthetic reference.

This flow doesn't need to produce new material by default. The focus is to deeply interpret the references, translate them, document them and turn the set into a clear base for the brand.
```

#### Brand primary colors

| Token | Hex | RGB | Primary use |

| `secondary` | #XXXXXX | R, G, B | Support, hover, secondary graphics |

**Why these primaries:** [paragraph: what sensation they evoke, contrast with the niche, connection with the brand's essence, visual references that justify]

#### Neutral tones

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `background-light` | #XXXXXX | Light background, email body, paper for printed pieces |
| `background-dark` | #XXXXXX | Dark background, hero, tension slides |
| `text-on-light` | #XXXXXX | Text over light background |
| `text-on-dark` | #XXXXXX | Text over dark background |
| `border` | #XXXXXX | Dividers, subtle outlines |
| `surface` | #XXXXXX | Cards, containers, elevated areas |

**Why these neutrals:** [paragraph — cool/neutral/warm, sensation they carry (clinical/digital vs. editorial/tactile)]

#### Semantic colors (UI)

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `success` | #16A34A (or customized) | Confirmations, positive actions, approved badges |
| `warning` | #EAB308 | Non-critical warnings, attention |
| `error` | #DC2626 | Errors, destructive actions, critical alerts |
| `info` | #2563EB | Informative messages, links, tips |

**Why these semantic tones:** [if WCAG standard: "I use WCAG convention because it facilitates universal recognition"; if customized to harmonize with brand palette: "derived from the primary palette for visual coherence in UI"]

#### Derived tones

Calculated from the primaries when necessary:

- `primary-light` = primary lightened ~20% (LCH) — subtle backgrounds
- `primary-dark` = primary darkened ~30% — hover states
- `primary-5` = primary with alpha 5% — very subtle background highlights
- `primary-15` = primary with alpha 15% — keyword highlights
- `primary-30` = primary with alpha 30% — colored borders

#### Application rules (when to use each color)

#### Visual usage percentage

| Token | Recommended percentage | Function |
|---|---:|---|
| `background-light` / `background-dark` | ...% | Dominant field, reading base |
| `text-on-light` / `text-on-dark` | ...% | Main information |
| `primary` | ...% | Identity, emphasis, CTA, memorable detail |
| `secondary` | ...% | Support, controlled variation |
| `accent` / derived tones | ...% | Micro-details, highlights, graphics |

**How to estimate:** combine reading of the sent materials with visual analysis of the pieces. The percentage isn't decorative math; it's practical instruction to prevent the brand from using too much color, too little color or color in the wrong place.

**`primary`:**
- ✅ In keywords within copy (1-3 per piece)
- ✅ As main CTA color
- ✅ As thin accent bar (4-7px) at top of slides
- ✅ As gradient base in strong hero
- ❌ NEVER as background of a large text block (tires the eye)
- ❌ NEVER in more than 3 consecutive words
- ❌ NEVER in more than 1 CTA per screen

**`background-dark`:**
- ✅ Hero of tension/revelation pieces
- ✅ Impact slides, mechanism moments
- ❌ NEVER in welcome pieces, celebratory contexts

**(Repeat for each relevant token.)**

#### Approved and forbidden combinations

✅ Approved combinations:
- `primary` over `background-dark` — high contrast, strong identity
- `primary` over `background-light` — calm accent

❌ Forbidden combinations:
- `primary` over `secondary` (if applicable) — visual noise
- (etc.)

#### Minimum contrast

Contrast is the reading difference between text and background. Write in practical language: which combinations the brand can use, which to avoid and why.

Mandatory format:

- **What it means:** [explain without jargon in 1 sentence]
- **How to apply:** [2-4 approved combinations]
- **Example:** [a real situation of post, site, email, packaging or presentation]
- **Avoid:** [combinations that look pretty but hinder reading or break the brand]

If citing WCAG, explain as a legibility rule, not as technical code. Never deliver just "WCAG AA — 4.5:1 / 3:1" without practical translation.

### 3.2 Typography

> Typography must be found and interpreted. When there's a site, PDF, deck, logo, screenshot or visual piece, the Maestro must look for typographic families, lettering styles, weights, contrast, spacing, upper/lowercase and editorial function. If the exact font name can't be confirmed, it must register the perceived family, the hypothesis and the limit.

| Token | Family | Weights | Primary use |
|---|---|---|---|
| `display` | [Family] | [weights: 100-900] | Headlines, covers, hero, giant numbers |
| `body` | [Family] | [weights: 100-900] | Paragraphs, captions, body text, UI |

**Why these fonts:** [paragraph — what sensation they evoke, what personality they carry, with which other brands they resonate, contrast with the niche]

| Token | Size | Line-height | Letter-spacing | Weight | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `display-xl` | 96-108px | 0.95 | -3px | 900 | Carousel cover, strong hero |
| `display-l` | 72-80px | 1.0 | -2px | 900 | Internal dark headline |
| `display-m` | 64-72px | 1.05 | -2px | 900 | Internal light headline |
| `display-s` | 48-56px | 1.1 | -1px | 800 | Section subtitles |
| `body-l` | 40-44px | 1.5 | 0 | 400 | Strong body, email hero |
| `body-m` | 36-40px | 1.5 | 0 | 400 | Standard body (carousel, post) |
| `body-s` | 28-32px | 1.55 | 0.2px | 400 | Caption, email body |
| `body-xs` | 18-22px | 1.6 | 0.4px | 400 | Web body, form |
| `tag` | 13-15px | 1.4 | 3px | 700 | Section tag (uppercase) |
| `mono-s` | 12-14px | 1.4 | 0.5px | 600 | Metadata, slide numbers |

#### Usage principles

- **Headline** always in `display`, bold/black weight, uppercase, negative kerning
- **Body** always in `body`, regular weight, sentence case, neutral kerning
- **Tag** always in `mono`, weight 600+, uppercase, positive letter-spacing
- **Never** same font/weight/size for two different hierarchical elements

#### Text color over each background

- **Over `background-dark`:** body in white 55% opacity, strong in white 100%, accent in `primary`
- **Over `background-light`:** body in black 60% opacity, strong in black 100%, accent in `primary`
- **Accent (`primary`)** appears only in keywords — never in entire sentences

### 3.4 Logo

**Existing variants** (in `referencias/`):
- `logo-primary` (horizontal) — standard use in headers, footers, hero
- `logo-vertical` — square spaces, social profiles
- `monogram` — favicon, app icon, small square contexts
- `logo-mono-white` — over dark backgrounds without brand color
- `logo-mono-black` — over light backgrounds without brand color

**Clear space:** minimum = 0.5 × the "x" height of the main word.

**Minimum size:**
- Digital: 80px width
- Print: 25mm
- Favicon: 16px (use `monogram`)

**Logo anti-use:**
- ❌ Rotated logo (rotation ≠ 0°)
- ❌ Distorted logo (altered proportion)
- ❌ Logo with filter/effect (strong drop shadow, glow, blur)
- ❌ Logo in unapproved color
- ❌ Logo below minimum size

**Extraction and use in the single PDF:**
- If the logo exists on the site, social network, PDF, mockup or sent file, the Maestro should try to extract the best available version and save it in `referencias/`.
- The logo should compose the `resultado/DNA.pdf` whenever quality allows.
- If extraction isn't possible or quality is poor, document the limit and continue with other brand signals.
- The absence of a logo should never halt the DNA or reduce the depth of the analysis.
- When there's a logo, it should guide proportion, breathing, visual rhythm, cover signature and section openings.

## Mandatory principles in generation

When the Maestro generates `resultado/DNA.md` and `resultado/DNA.pdf`, it follows these principles:

Before rendering the PDF, the Maestro must also apply `18-Design-Director.md`. This file is the document's visual lock and takes priority over any rendering that looks like markdown, report or poorly-laid-out page.

### 0. It's not a report: it's a well-proven story

The single PDF must read like a clear essay on the brand. It can contain deep analysis, but it can't look like a spreadsheet, cold report or technical list. The Maestro's job is to transform evidence into understanding.

Quality criteria:

- If it only describes what exists, it's incomplete
- If it doesn't explain context, it's loose
- If it doesn't show what the references mean, it's shallow
- If it doesn't translate visual, voice and behavior into simple language, it's too technical
- If it doesn't form a coherent story from start to finish, it's poorly edited
- If a marketing person doesn't understand without help, it's poorly written

### 0.1 Mandatory language

The final DNA must use simple, direct and human language.

Writing rules:

- Complete sentences, with beginning, middle and end
- Every visible sentence starts with capital letter
- Correct punctuation in titles, bullets, captions, tables and PDF
- Short paragraphs, but not choppy
- No em dash
- No jargon without explanation
- No truncated sentence
- No loose fragment that looks like internal note
- No long sentence trying to explain everything at once
- Prefer common words to technical terms
- Explain unavoidable terms in simple language
- Write like a well-edited essay, not loose bullets
- Keep the text contextual, directed and standardized

Didactic rule: whoever reads the DNA has no obligation to know branding, design, typography, photography, UI, UX, contrast or technical terms. Each guideline needs to explain what to do and why it matters. Bad example: `Two accents on the same piece.` Correct example: `Avoid using two accent colors on the same piece, because they compete with each other and leave the composition confusing. Choose one highlight color at a time.`

The document's tone must be safe, precise and faithful to the brand studied. If the brand is aggressive, acidic, mocking, provocative, sexual, popular, rough, institutional, delicate, technical, punk, mocking or uses profanity, the DNA must record this without softening. Depth comes from the reading of evidence, not from difficult words.

### 0.2 Forensic authenticity of personality

The Maestro is not a censor, polisher or consultant trying to make the brand more acceptable. The Maestro is a direct, straight and forensic analyst. It documents the personality as it is observed and as it must operate.

Rules:

- Do not soften the brand's real personality due to personal taste, generic prudence or abstract taboo
- Do not turn aggressive brand into "safe", mocking brand into "friendly", explicit brand into "elegant" or popular brand into "premium"
- If the brand uses profanity, record which it uses, with what frequency, in what context, with what intent and where it works
- If the brand provokes, confronts, ironizes or pressures, document the mechanism with precision
- If the brand has force, anger, urgency, tension, sensuality, acidic humor or brute language, this enters the DNA as identity trait
- Separate "doesn't fit the brand" from "I would avoid". The first is analysis. The second doesn't belong in the DNA
- Only mark as a limit what is evidence of the brand itself, legal restriction, platform restriction, real commercial risk or explicit user request
- When there's tension between authenticity and risk, document the tension. Don't erase the authenticity

### 1. Every choice has justification

No color, font, principle, word enters without **a short paragraph explaining why**. Justification enables coherent decision-making in new situations by the agents/people who will produce content later.

### 2. Semantic colors always present

Even if the person didn't ask, the Maestro suggests `success/warning/error/info`. Can be:
- **WCAG standard** (#16A34A success, #EAB308 warning, #DC2626 error, #2563EB info) — more legible in UI, universal
- **Derived from brand palette** — more visually cohesive, sacrifices a little UI legibility

The person chooses, but the section always exists.

### 3. Derived tones always present

Even if the person only gave the primary, the Maestro calculates and documents `primary-light`, `primary-dark`, `primary-5`, `primary-15`, `primary-30`. Other pieces will need them.

### 4. Complete typographic hierarchy always present

Even if the person only cited the display font, the Maestro proposes complete scale (display-xl through mono-s) and asks for validation. System without complete hierarchy forces improvisation later.

### 5. Application rules are as important as tokens

Defining `primary = #X` is half the work. The other half is "when to use primary, when NEVER use primary, which other colors it combines with". Without this, the next AI uses primary as block background and breaks the brand.

### 6. Tones modulated by context in the voice

Brand speaks differently in caption vs. transactional email vs. crisis. Maestro maps 5-7 typical contexts and defines predominant tone of each, with opening example.

### 7. Where the person didn't answer, propose based on reasoning

If the person didn't define semantic colors, Maestro **proposes** with justification based on her palette and asks for validation. Doesn't leave `[to be defined]` in operational things — only in administrative data (name, @, URL).

### 8. References, links and images are part of the DNA

Everything that's in `referencias/` and everything listed in `discovery/link-inventory.md` and `discovery/asset-inventory.md` must be considered before generation: site, Instagram, LinkedIn, portfolio, blog, newsletter, PDFs, decks, saved links, competitors, aspirational references, anti-references, images, logos, palettes, fonts, texts, photos, videos, VFX, mockups, screenshots, selfies and loose materials.

When there are links or files, the Maestro needs to do deep analysis before writing the DNA: follow relevant internal pages, compare channels, map recurrences, identify inconsistencies and record access limits. This analysis generates `discovery/link-analysis.md`, `discovery/asset-analysis.md`, `discovery/dna-observado.md` and `discovery/gaps-e-hipoteses.md`. The `DNA.md` must incorporate these findings into the "Sources studied" section and use the observed hypotheses to justify strategy, voice and visual.

When there's a site, the analysis must include an x-ray of the site: pages navigated, texts read, images interpreted, journey structure, tone of voice, behavior, promise, proof, objections, conversion, margins, breathing, grid and visual rhythm. The site must be read as an intimate manifestation of the brand, not as a loose page.

In sites rich in images, the Maestro must create `discovery/site-visual-inventory.md` and visually analyze a volume proportional to the material found. The floor is 12 relevant images when that volume is available. In sites with large visual repertoire, the expected is to study 20 to 60 images, including hero, product, people, ambient, mockups, icons, illustrations, banners, cases, applications and video frames. If a clearly visual site generates only one logo and a few images, the analysis is incomplete and needs to be redone or justified by access limit.

If there are images, videos, VFX or mockups, they need to appear in the reasoning of the `DNA.md` with explicit analysis of composition, light, palette, visual color usage percentage, texture, typography, atmosphere, language, motion, rhythm and usage instructions. The `resultado/DNA.pdf` needs to incorporate these materials as editorial part of the document, not just as loose attachment. This is the only final PDF of the flow.

Photos and typography are mandatory in the analysis when there are signals available. The Maestro must look for photos in site, networks, portfolio, PDFs, decks, screenshots, mockups and sent files. Must also look for typography in public CSS, images, PDFs, logos, screenshots and editorial materials. If it doesn't find, it needs to record clearly "not found" and explain where it looked.

The visual reading must be intense. The Maestro must analyze how things happen visually: how the photo is made, how the light enters, where there's shadow, what projections appear, how people pose, how products are held, supported, packaged, brought close or isolated, how services are represented, what surfaces appear, what depth of field exists, what texture dominates, what kind of crop and edit repeats, and what editorial line this builds.

If the user sends images that contain instructions, tables or content examples, the Maestro must read these images as text and structure. They may define what the DNA needs to contain. This does not mean using the visual aesthetic of these images as PDF identity.

Mandatory content checklist when applicable:

- **Reference types:** brands, directors/photographers, design/editorial, cinema and culture
- **Tone of voice:** general tone, rhythm, vocabulary, avoid, humor and positioning
- **Visual:** palette, aesthetics, composition, light, texture and references
- **Photography:** direction, lighting, shadow, projection, pose, product/service, setting, perceived lens, treatment and editorial line
- **Typography:** families, lettering, weights, scale, hierarchy, contrast, spacing, function and personality

Each item needs to leave the level of "guide question" and become extracted reading of the brand: what was observed, what it means, how to apply and what to avoid.

### 9. The PDF absorbs the brand

The `resultado/DNA.pdf` must follow the visual base extracted from the brand whenever possible. This includes logo, typography, colors, margins, breathing, empty spaces, visual density, grid, image proportion, cover style, section openings and graphic details.

The document should not use a generic template if there's enough material to understand the brand. It should look like a natural continuation of the analyzed identity. If the logo exists and has sufficient quality, it should appear in the PDF. If it doesn't exist, the Maestro records this and composes the document with the other available visual signals.

Don't use ASCII color squares, improvised markings or appearance of exported report. Palette must be shown in real color blocks, images need to have visual study layout, and the page must have deliberate composition.

Visual gallery isn't an attachment. It must look like a magazine spread or presentation: hero image, mosaics, crops, study pages, discreet captions and volume proportional to the material. If the site has 20 relevant images, the PDF can't show only 3. The minimum acceptable is to reflect the extracted visual abundance or explain real technical limit.

The Maestro must act as a designer before acting as automatic layout machine. Long text becomes cards, lists, columns, tables or additional pages. Never deliver tangled paragraph, glued, without hierarchy or without rhythm. Images must preserve proportion by default. Cropping is only allowed when it's a clear aesthetic decision and should never mutilate product, face, lettering, packaging or important information.

Each page must pass basic design criteria: consistent margin, real breathing between image block and text block, rule of thirds when there's visual narrative, clear contrast between title, body, caption and metadata, contextualization of what's being displayed and typography aligned with the brand or a justifiable approximation when the exact font isn't available.

Aesthetics isn't finish. Aesthetics, design system, appearance, layout, good visual, elegance and sense of care represent most of the perception of PDF quality. If the document looks ugly, poor, sad, sloppy, generic or poorly laid out, it has failed, even if the text is correct. The Maestro must revise and render again until the material looks like a high-level visual presentation.

Text can never look like markdown in the PDF. Bullets need to be laid out as bullets: each item with its own line, breathing, visual marker, numbering, card or column. Never turn a list into running paragraph, never leave visible markdown symbols, never paste bullets on a single line.

The PDF must be direct and consolidated. The `DNA.md` can be more complete and operational, but the `DNA.pdf` needs to transform depth into intelligent reading. Don't create giant, crude or empty-space-filled manual. Don't move important information just to produce volume. The presentation must prioritize synthesis, clear bullets, numbered lists, matrices, visual spreads and short well-defined paragraphs.

### 10. Interpretation proves the system

Every complete DNA must show how the references were interpreted. The Maestro needs to translate every important set of materials into meaning, rule and implication for the brand. It's not mandatory to produce new pieces, images or campaigns. The most important thing is to document well what the references teach.

### 11. Where the person didn't answer AND the Maestro has no way to propose, leave it explicit

In strategic/identity things only the person can define, leave `[to be defined]` or `[under construction]` — never invent.
