# 6 — Anti-references (what you do NOT want to be)

This is one of the most important folders of the briefing — and the one most people forget.

**Drop here anything that represents what you do NOT want to look like.** As important as positive inspiration.

**It can be:**
- Screenshot of a brand whose visuals bother you
- Graphic style you find dated/saturated
- Type of copy you recognize as "the brand that loses you"
- Visual cliché you want to avoid at all cost (purple AI tech gradient, lightbulb as an idea, blue SaaS dashboard)

**Why it matters so much:** identity is built as much by "this is ours" as by "this never". Without a clear anti-reference, it's easy to fall into the generic — because the generic is where everything ends up if you don't know what you're avoiding.

**You can also write text:** if writing is easier than screenshotting, create a file `what-i-dont-want.txt` here and list it. Like:
- "I don't want to look like a motivational marketing course"
- "I don't want dated 2010s typography"
- "I don't want team photos smiling in a meeting room"
- "I don't want copy with 'discover how'"
